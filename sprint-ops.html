Sprint Ops – Unified Operations Platform for Airline Staff

Project
Sprint Ops was envisioned as a unified, mobile-friendly operations hub to centralize critical airport workflows. Flydubai’s airport teams relied on disconnected legacy tools—an outdated desktop cashiering app, Excel-based operational logs, and manual INAD case handling. These fragmented workflows led to inefficiencies, compliance risks, delayed reporting, and high cognitive load for frontline staff.The platform focused on automation, real-time visibility, and role-based experiences to improve efficiency and compliance. Designed for scale, Sprint Ops supports future global rollouts and the seamless addition of new operational modules.
The challenge

The challenge was to design a centralized, mobile-friendly platform ("Sprint Ops") consolidating mission-critical modules, promoting automation, compliance, and a seamless user experience while remaining scalable for future global rollouts.

Scope

Mobile design(RWD),Web app design

Tools

Figma, FigJam, Advanced AI Tools

Team

Timeline

Nov 2023 - Oct 2025

Design Methodolgy

For Sprint Ops, we used a Lean UX approach with quick Make → Check → Adjust cycles, testing ideas directly with airport agents and supervisors. Each module—Cashier, INAD, Concierge, and Operational Log—was treated as a assumptions to validate through real feedback rather than a fixed set of requirements.

Discovery Phase
  • On-ground observations with different User persona’s persona’s including cashiers, ground agents, concierge staff, supervisors, and Hub Control to understand how they work in real situations
  • We used empathy mapping during shadowing sessions across check-in, boarding, cashier, and INAD (Inadmissible Passenger) areas to understand what users think, feel, say, and do in their real operational workflows.
  • Data collection from existing systems, reports, error logs, and manual registers to understand volume, frequency, and pain points in current processes.
Discovery Phase insights
  • High manual workload: Frequent manual entries for payments, INAD data, and log events caused errors and delays.
  • Fragmented tools: Agents switched between 3–6 systems per shift—raising cognitive load and slowing tasks.
  • Slow reporting: Supervisors struggled with collecting and validating shift reports and compliance data.
  • Mobility needs: Agents needed mobile-first access as many tasks occur while moving between counters
Problem Statement

As an airport agent, I need a single, intuitive platform for all my operational workflows, so I can serve passengers efficiently and maintain regulatory compliance without unnecessary friction.

Design Goals
  • Unified platform: Consolidate all critical airport operations into a single, mobile-friendly application.
  • Role-based experiences: Tailor interfaces and workflows to specific user roles (cashiers, ground agents, supervisors) for efficiency.
  • Automation: Minimize manual data entry through intelligent defaults, auto-fill, and system integrations.
  • Real-time visibility: Provide supervisors with dashboards for live monitoring of operations and compliance status.
  • Scalability: Design modular components that can be easily expanded for future global rollouts and additional functionalities.
Final Solution Highlights
  • Role-based navigation ensures users see only modules and data relevant to their duties, minimizing distraction and error
  • Responsive dashboards show KPIs, live status, and overdue alerts across modules (payments, INADs, concierge tasks, shift logs).
  • Search and filter tools enable supervisors and agents to find cases, logs, and reports instantly, even across large data sets.
  • Accessible design: Clear type hierarchy, high-contrast theme, and mobile optimization.
  • Export/report features allow easy sharing with finance and compliance, in Excel/PDF formats.
Measured Impact
  • 40% reduction in manual entry errors (measured one month post-launch).
  • Median transaction time on payment and INAD modules decreased by 30%.
  • 3x faster shift handover and event reporting by operations teams.
  • Improved staff satisfaction and streamlined onboarding due to consistent, predictable UX.
Key Learnings
Iterative collaboration is essential

Direct testing with airport staff revealed workflow nuances not visible in documents or SOPs.

Modular design accelerates scalability

Each module functions independently but shares a common design language, enabling future expansion.

Role-adaptive navigation is crucial

Different roles require different information density—clarifying this dramatically improved usability and reduced overwhelm.

Lightweight UI in low bandwidth-limited

Minimal icons and fewer heavy assets improves performance and ensured fast load times in airport systems.

Design System

The design system—covering colour palettes, typography, spacing, iconography, and reusable UI components—was fully aligned with flyDubai’s brand guidelines. This ensured a consistent visual identity across all modules and screens, while also helping airport teams quickly recognize patterns and navigate with confidence.

Conclusion

Sprint Ops successfully unified multiple critical airport workflows into a single operational hub, using user-centered research, modular architecture, and iterative design to streamline operations, support compliance, and reduce cognitive load for flydubai’s airport teams today and in future expansions. Consistent interaction patterns across modules—search and filters, validation, attachments, and dashboards—proved just as important as individual screen polish, making onboarding easier in a high-pressure environment. With accessible layouts and responsive design, the platform now helps airport teams make faster, more confident decisions while maintaining service quality, even as processes and regulations continue to evolve.
